# THE GRAND MASTER — Master Index & Reading Map

*A navigation map for the ten knowledge codices. For each domain: the **core asset** (the single variable everything hangs on — where a lesson or a debate should begin), the **core clashes** (the contested questions the codex is built around, and the ready motions), and the **key works & thinkers** cited (the primary sources for anyone who wishes to read past the codex). Use it to route the motion generator, to plan a lecture, or to find the right codex fast.*

---

## 01 — History's Turning Points
**Core asset:** agency vs. structure — did the individual move history, or the tide that would have thrown up someone like him?
**Core clashes:** disciplined counterfactual vs. convenient fantasy · turning point vs. mere acceleration · net assessment (good *for whom*, over *what timeframe*) · presentism vs. legitimate cross-time judgment · single-cause history vs. overdetermination.
**Ready motions:** printing press vs. political revolution · regret the Industrial Revolution · contingency vs. structure · decolonization's balance · forager vs. early farmer.
**Key works:** Carlyle (*On Heroes* — Great Man) · Braudel & the Annales (*longue durée*) · Diamond (*Guns, Germs, and Steel*) · Acemoglu–Robinson (*Why Nations Fail*) · Weber (*The Protestant Ethic*) · Gould (contingency) · Ferguson (*Virtual History*) · Butterfield (Whig history) · Crosby (the Columbian Exchange) · Eisenstein (the printing press) · Pomeranz (*The Great Divergence*) · Maddison (long-run GDP).

## 02 — Global Economy: Trade, Development & Growth
**Core asset:** incentives and who bears the cost — and *state capacity* as the hidden variable behind every "just copy the policy."
**Core clashes:** openness vs. protection (and the matter of *sequencing* / export discipline) · growth vs. distribution · state vs. market · correlation vs. causation (the China question).
**Ready motions:** free trade vs. foreign aid · industrial policy for late development · manufacturing vs. services · inequality as the price of growth · unilateral tariff abolition.
**Key works:** Ricardo (comparative advantage) · Smith · Hamilton & List (infant industry) · Chang (*Kicking Away the Ladder*) · Johnson (*MITI*), Amsden, Wade (developmental state) · Solow & Romer (growth) · Acemoglu–Robinson (institutions) · Prebisch–Singer (dependency/terms of trade) · Autor–Dorn–Hanson (the China shock) · Banerjee–Duflo–Kremer (RCTs) · Lin (new structural economics) · Piketty (inequality) · Stiglitz (IMF critique).

## 03 — Geopolitics & the Changing World Order
**Core asset:** anarchy and self-help — there is no one to call, so every state provides its own security.
**Core clashes:** multipolarity — stabilizing balance or war-prone miscalculation · do institutions constrain the strong (conditionally) · the Thucydides Trap — risk or destiny · align vs. hedge · is the liberal order worth defending.
**Ready motions:** multipolar vs. unipolar danger · EU strategic autonomy · US–China conflict · middle-power alignment · NATO expansion regret.
**Key works:** Thucydides (Melian Dialogue) · Morgenthau · Waltz (neorealism) · Mearsheimer (offensive realism) · Keohane (institutionalism) · Nye (soft power) · Doyle (democratic peace) · Wendt (constructivism) · Bull (English School) · Allison (Thucydides Trap) · Jervis (security dilemma; spiral vs. deterrence) · Wohlforth · Gaddis (the long peace) · Fukuyama (end of history).

## 04 — Law, Justice & Rights
**Core asset:** order vs. liberty — and who binds the binder (the leash on the person *and* the leash on the state).
**Core clashes:** purpose of punishment · judicial review and the counter-majoritarian difficulty · free speech (harm vs. the neutral rule) · rule of law thin vs. thick · duty to obey an unjust law.
**Ready motions:** rehabilitation vs. retribution · courts striking down laws · protecting abhorrent speech · rule of law as neutral good · duty to obey unjust law.
**Key works:** Kant/Hegel (retribution), Bentham (deterrence), Braithwaite (restorative) · Hart (positivism; aim/distribution), Kelsen, Austin, Fuller (inner morality), Dworkin (integrity, rights as trumps), Finnis (natural law), Raz & Bingham (rule of law) · Bickel (counter-majoritarian difficulty), Waldron (against review; hate speech), Ely (representation-reinforcing) · Mill (harm principle), Popper (paradox of tolerance), Holmes (marketplace of ideas), Posner (law & economics).

## 05 — Democracy, Governance & Institutions
**Core asset:** representation vs. governability — the mirror that reflects the crowd vs. the hammer that drives the nail.
**Core clashes:** PR vs. FPTP · compulsory voting · populism (demands vs. methods) · federalism vs. the centre · competence vs. legitimacy (technocracy) · tyranny of the majority.
**Ready motions:** PR vs. FPTP governance · compulsory voting · populism as symptom · federal vs. central for diverse societies · insulating decisions from majorities.
**Key works:** Duverger (law) · Lijphart (majoritarian vs. consensus; consociationalism) · Mudde (populism as thin ideology) · Levitsky–Ziblatt (*How Democracies Die* — backsliding) · Arrow (impossibility theorem) · Buchanan–Tullock (public choice) · Dahl (polyarchy) · Habermas (deliberative) · Schumpeter (minimalist democracy).

## 06 — Political Philosophy & Ethics
**Core asset:** the good vs. the right — "what produces the most good?" vs. "what am I permitted to do?"
**Core clashes:** equality of outcome vs. opportunity · paternalism and the harm principle · Rawls vs. Nozick · duties to future generations · duties to distant strangers.
**Ready motions:** outcome vs. opportunity · state and self-harm · Rawlsian vs. libertarian society · rights of future generations · duty to distant strangers.
**Key works:** Bentham & Mill (utilitarianism; *On Liberty*) · Kant (categorical imperative) · Aristotle (virtue, *eudaimonia*) · Rawls (veil of ignorance, difference principle) · Nozick (entitlement, Wilt Chamberlain) · Sen & Nussbaum (capabilities) · Walzer (spheres of justice) · Berlin (two liberties) · Williams (integrity objection) · Parfit (non-identity) · Singer (beneficence) · Hobbes/Locke/Rousseau (social contract).

## 07 — War, Security & Just War
**Core asset:** when may you kill — the two questions of *jus ad bellum* (may you fight) and *jus in bello* (how).
**Core clashes:** intervention without UN authorization · nuclear deterrence and tail risk · negotiating with terrorists · autonomous weapons · the moral equality of combatants · torture / the ticking bomb.
**Ready motions:** humanitarian intervention without the Security Council · nuclear deterrence made us safer · never negotiate with terrorists · ban lethal autonomous weapons · soldiers on the unjust side · torture never justified.
**Key works:** Augustine & Aquinas · Vitoria & Suárez (School of Salamanca) · Grotius (*On the Law of War and Peace*) · Walzer (*Just and Unjust Wars*) · McMahan (*Killing in War* — revisionist) · Brodie & Schelling (deterrence) · R2P / the ICISS report · Chenoweth & Stephan (civil resistance) · Sparrow (autonomous weapons) · Gandhi & King (nonviolence).

## 08 — Technology & Society Governance
**Core asset:** innovation vs. precaution — the Collingridge dilemma (shapeable while blind; clear once too late).
**Core clashes:** strict liability for AI · breaking up platforms (network effects & data moats) · mass surveillance and the base-rate problem · automation and labor · intermediary liability & the myth of the neutral platform · the right to be forgotten · AI safety/existential risk · the global internet vs. the splinternet.
**Ready motions:** strict liability for AI harms · break up dominant platforms · mass surveillance to prevent terror · automation harming developing economies · abolish intermediary shields · right to be forgotten · mandatory AI safety regulation · global platforms vs. splinternet.
**Key works:** Collingridge (the dilemma) · Winner ("Do Artifacts Have Politics?") · Pinch & Bijker (SCOT) · Bork (consumer-welfare standard) · Khan & Wu (neo-Brandeisian antitrust) · Westin (privacy as control) · Nissenbaum (contextual integrity) · Zuboff (surveillance capitalism) · Keynes (technological unemployment) · Autor (task framework) · Solow (productivity paradox) · Bostrom & Russell (AI safety) · Jevons (paradox).

## 09 — Religion, Secularism & the State
**Core asset:** two rival neutralities — the state neutral by *excluding* religion vs. neutral by engaging all faiths *even-handedly*.
**Core clashes:** strict secularism vs. accommodation · religious exemptions & third-party harm · accommodation in diverse democracies · community autonomy & minorities-within-minorities · religious symbols on public officials · state reform of religious practice · public reason.
**Ready motions:** exclude religious reasoning from law · religious exemptions from general laws · accommodation vs. strict separation · community control of personal law · ban religious symbols on officials · state reform of discriminatory practices.
**Key works:** Kuru (assertive vs. passive secularism) · Bhargava (principled distance — the Indian model) · Rawls (public reason) · Wolterstorff (the critique of public reason) · Habermas (translation) · Okin ("Is Multiculturalism Bad for Women?" — minorities within minorities).

## 10 — Environment & Climate Policy
**Core asset:** the commons and the free rider — the shared pasture ruined by each shepherd acting sensibly.
**Core clashes:** carbon price vs. subsidy · nuclear vs. renewables-plus-storage (firm vs. intermittent power) · climate finance / reparations (CBDR) · adaptation vs. mitigation · developing-world fossil use & energy poverty · degrowth vs. green growth · geoengineering · individual vs. systemic responsibility.
**Ready motions:** carbon pricing vs. subsidy · nuclear as primary path · climate reparations · adaptation over mitigation · fossil use to end energy poverty · degrowth vs. green growth · deploy solar geoengineering · individual action as distraction.
**Key works:** Stern vs. Nordhaus (the discount-rate divide) · Weitzman (fat-tailed catastrophic risk) · Daly (ecological economics / steady state) · Mazzucato (green industrial policy) · Hickel & Jackson (degrowth) · Hardin (tragedy of the commons) · Ostrom (*Governing the Commons* — the counter to fatalism) · Pigou (externalities / the carbon price).

## How the pieces fit
- **Core asset** → where to *begin* a lesson or frame a debate (isolate this variable first; everything else hangs on it).
- **Core clashes** → the contested questions to *test* a student on, or to spin a motion from.
- **Key works** → the *primary reading* to send anyone who wants past the codex to the source.

Each codex then carries, in full: the competing schools (claim → mechanism → strongest case → standard objection), the factual record, the debates with arguments and rebuttals on *both* sides, the myths and factual traps, and the adjudicator's fact-check notes.
